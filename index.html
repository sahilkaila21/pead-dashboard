Indicators & entry/exit rules

What actually drives a trade — no price-chart technicals, no sentiment/NLP; the whole signal is earnings surprise

Entry trigger

Earnings surprise % (reported EPS vs. analyst estimate) exceeds +15% — roughly the 80th percentile of surprises across the universe, i.e. the largest beats, not just any beat.

Reaction-date timing

Reports before ~noon react the same trading day; reports after-close react the next trading day. Entry executes at that day's close — deliberately after the initial jump, capturing only the follow-on drift.

Position sizing

ATR (14-day Average True Range) sets the initial stop distance; shares are sized so the stop, if hit, loses exactly 1% of current equity — bigger ATR (more volatile stock) means fewer shares.

Stop-loss

Chandelier exit: stop = highest close since entry − 2.5× ATR. Ratchets up only, never down. Checked before any other exit, every day.

Hold period / exit

Fixed 20 trading-day hold (the horizon where the drift effect was strongest and most statistically significant in the research phase), or the stop, whichever comes first. No profit-target exit — cutting the hold short would cut the drift the trade is betting on.

Ranking under the position cap

Up to 15 concurrent positions (earnings cluster heavily by calendar week). When more than 15 signals fire the same day, the largest surprises get priority, not whichever ticker is alphabetically first.
